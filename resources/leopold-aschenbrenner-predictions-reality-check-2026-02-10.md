## Meta-Analysis: What Aschenbrenner Got Right and Wrong

### What He Got Right

1. **Compute scaling trajectory**: Dramatically underestimated if anything - $100-500B Stargate vs his $10B prediction
2. **AI revenue growth**: On track to exceed $100B combined annual revenue
3. **Capability improvements**: College graduate-level AI achieved on schedule
4. **China competitiveness**: DeepSeek validated prediction despite export controls
5. **Taiwan tensions**: Escalating exactly as predicted, converging with AGI timelines
6. **Algorithmic efficiency**: 30% annual hardware cost reduction sustained
7. **Automated engineering**: 77% SWE-bench, production deployments at scale
8. **AI-accelerated research**: Autonomous labs operational, 10x+ speedups demonstrated

### What He Underestimated

1. **Test-time compute scaling**: o1/o3 reasoning paradigm provides capability gains independent of parameter scaling - a major breakthrough he didn't emphasize
2. **Speed of infrastructure buildout**: Gigawatt-scale clusters operational in 2025, not 2027-28
3. **Open-weight model progress**: Gap from 8% to 1.7% in single year - catching up faster than expected

### What He Got Wrong (So Far)

1. **AI lab security**: Deteriorating not improving - first AI-orchestrated espionage, major breaches, no government-mandated security improvements
2. **Government AGI project timeline**: Emerging but not yet materialized as formal "Manhattan Project" - may be on track for 2027-28 but not accelerated

### The Key Uncertainty: Takeoff Speed

Aschenbrenner's core thesis is **fast takeoff** - once models reach a certain capability threshold, recursive self-improvement accelerates dramatically.

**Evidence for fast takeoff**:
- Google AI Co-Scientist: 48 hours = 10 years of human research
- Autonomous labs: 10x data collection speed
- Frontier labs automating operations, effective workforce scaling to hundreds of thousands

**Evidence against (or not yet)**:
- Benchmark performance ≠ autonomous reliability (developers retain control)
- Gap between "can complete task" and "can be trusted unsupervised"
- Recursive improvement still in early stages, not yet runaway
