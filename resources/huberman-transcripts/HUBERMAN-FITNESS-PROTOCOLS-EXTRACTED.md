# Huberman Lab Fitness Protocols - Extracted Actionable Protocols

> Extracted from: Fitness Toolkit (Protocol & Tools to Optimize Physical Health) + Science-Supported Tools to Accelerate Your Fitness Goals

## 1. WEEKLY SCHEDULE - FOUNDATIONAL FITNESS PROTOCOL

| Day | Focus | Duration | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 1 (Sun)** | Long endurance / Zone 2 cardio | 60-75 min jog OR 2.5-5 hr hike | Cardiovascular |
| **Day 2 (Mon)** | Legs - quads, hamstrings, calves | 50-60 min work (after 10 min warmup) | Resistance |
| **Day 3 (Tue)** | Heat/cold contrast - recovery | 3-5 rounds (60-100 min total) | Recovery |
| **Day 4 (Wed)** | Torso - chest, shoulders, back, neck | 50-60 min work (after 10 min warmup) | Resistance |
| **Day 5 (Thu)** | Moderate-intensity cardio | 30-35 min at 75-80% max effort | Cardiovascular |
| **Day 6 (Fri)** | HIIT - sprint intervals | 8-12 rounds | Cardiovascular |
| **Day 7 (Sat)** | Arms, calves, neck + indirect torso | 45-60 min | Resistance |

**Scheduling rules:**
- Any workout can shift 1 day forward or 1 day back to accommodate real life
- Never place HIIT the day after legs (minimum 2-3 days spacing)
- Each muscle group hit 1x directly + 1x indirectly per week
- Every 12-16 weeks, take 1 full week off (light hiking/activity only)

## 2. ZONE 2 CARDIO

**Target:** 150-200 min per week minimum (Huberman recommends 200 min)

**Heart rate test:** You can hold a conversation. If you pushed any harder, you could not complete sentences. Alternatively, you can maintain purely nasal breathing the entire time.

**How to accumulate:**
- 1 dedicated session per week: 60-90 min jog or 1-4 hr hike
- Remaining minutes: fast walking, taking stairs, walking meetings, carrying groceries, pacing during phone calls
- Zone 2 does NOT impede strength/hypertrophy - it enhances other fitness domains
- Does NOT need to be scheduled as formal exercise

**Weight vest option:** 10-50 lbs for hikes/walks to increase effort at same duration

**Progressive overload:** As fitness improves, you must move faster or add weight to stay in Zone 2

---

## 3. MAX HEART RATE FORMULA

**Formula:** 220 - your age = estimated max heart rate

**HIIT target:** Get to near-max heart rate at least 1x per week

---

## 4. HIIT PROTOCOL (Friday)

**Equipment:** Assault bike/Airdyne bike (preferred for safety), rower, or sprint/jog intervals

**Protocol:**
- 3-5 min warmup (jogging, jumping jacks, jump rope)
- 20-30 sec all-out sprint (arms + legs on assault bike)
- 10 sec rest
- Repeat for 8-12 rounds
- Cool down: walk until breathing recovers

**Safety:** Do NOT go 100% all-out on running sprints. Stay at ~95% to avoid injury (sciatica, pelvic floor strain). Assault bike/rower are safer because they constrain range of motion.

---

## 5. THE SUGARCANE PROTOCOL (from Andy Galpin/Kenny Kane)

**Frequency:** Once every 2-4 weeks, replacing normal HIIT session

**Structure:**
1. **Warm-up:** 3-5 min jogging/jumping jacks/jump rope
2. **Round 1:** Go maximum distance in exactly 2 minutes. Record distance (e.g., 600m).
3. **Rest:** 2 minutes
4. **Round 2:** Cover the same distance from Round 1 as fast as possible. Record time (e.g., 2 min 30 sec).
5. **Rest:** 2 minutes
6. **Round 3:** Go all out for the same duration as Round 2 (e.g., 2 min 30 sec). Goal: match or exceed Round 1 distance. Continue all out until time expires.
7. **Cool down:** Walk until breathing recovers. Do NOT just collapse.

---

## 6. MODERATE CARDIO SESSION (Thursday)

- **Duration:** 30-35 min after 5-10 min warmup
- **Intensity:** 75-80% of maximum effort
- **Breathing:** Hard enough that nasal-only breathing is not possible
- **Mode:** Running, rowing, cycling, stair climbing, burpees, jumping jacks
- **Purpose:** Cuts across multiple endurance fuel systems (not pure Zone 2, not HIIT)

## 7. RESISTANCE TRAINING - SETS, REPS, REST

### Monthly Periodization (alternating blocks)

| Variable | Month A (Strength) | Month B (Hypertrophy) |
|---|---|---|
| Reps per set | 4-8 | 8-15 |
| Sets per exercise | 3-5 | 2-3 |
| Rest between sets | 2-4 min | 60-90 sec (up to 2.5 min) |
| Weight | Heavier | Moderate |
| Duration | ~4 weeks | ~4 weeks |

**Alternate Month A and Month B continuously throughout the year.**

**Key rules:**
- 2 exercises per muscle group (3 max)
- 1 exercise = peak contraction / shortened position (e.g., leg extension, cable crossover, seated calf raise, leg curl)
- 1 exercise = stretch / lengthened position (e.g., hack squat, incline press, standing calf raise, pull-up)
- Total workout: 50-60 min of real work (past 60 min, cortisol rises and impedes recovery)
- 10 min warmup before work sets
- Hypertrophy range is actually 5-30 reps, but varying is essential to prevent plateaus
- ~30% of sets to failure. Remaining sets: hard effort, struggling on final reps

### The 3x5 Protocol (Andy Galpin) - Pure Strength Block

**Duration:** 8-12 weeks per year (ideally 12 weeks)

**Structure - everything revolves around the number 3-5:**
- **3-5 exercises** per workout
- **3-5 sets** per exercise
- **3-5 reps** per set
- **3-5 minutes rest** between sets
- Can be done **3-5 times per week** (but adjust to your recovery)

**Exception:** Small muscle groups (rear delts, neck, calves) - use 5-8 reps instead of 3-5

**Warm-up for 3x5:**
1. Set 1: 6-8 reps very light (familiarize with movement)
2. Set 2: 4-6 reps with some load
3. Set 3 (optional, especially early in workout): 2-4 reps heavier
4. Then begin work sets at 3-5 reps

**Benefits documented:**
- Strength gains that persist when returning to higher rep ranges
- Improved cardiovascular output (stronger muscles = better running/rowing form)
- Reduced soreness compared to higher rep training
- More mental energy post-workout
- Offsets age-related strength decline (3-5% per year after age 40)

### Age-Related Decline Rates (after age 40, per year)

| Attribute | Annual Decline | Offset Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Muscle size | 1% per year | 6-10 working sets per muscle group per week minimum |
| Strength & power | 3-5% per year | Dedicated 3-5 rep strength training (12 weeks/year) |
| Speed & explosiveness | 8-10% per year | Speed/power specific training |

## 8. EXERCISE SELECTION PRINCIPLE

For each muscle group, choose:
1. **One peak contraction exercise** (muscle shortened under load)
2. **One stretch/lengthened exercise** (muscle loaded in stretched position)

**Examples:**

| Muscle Group | Peak Contraction | Stretch/Lengthened |
|---|---|---|
| Quads | Leg extension | Hack squat |
| Hamstrings | Leg curl | Glute-ham raise |
| Calves | Seated calf raise | Standing calf raise |
| Chest | Cable crossover | Incline press |
| Back | Seated row / bent-over row | Chin-up / pull-up |
| Biceps | Dumbbell curl | Incline curl |
| Triceps | Kickback | Overhead extension |

---

## 9. MIND-MUSCLE CONNECTION

**During sets:** Focus attention on the muscle being worked. Mentally isolate and contract it.
- Focus on moving the weight = shifts toward strength gains
- Focus on feeling the muscle = shifts toward hypertrophy

**Self-test:** If you can contract a muscle hard enough to feel a cramp WITHOUT any weight, you will likely grow that muscle easily. Muscles you cannot isolate mentally are harder to develop.

---

## 10. PHYSIOLOGICAL SIGH - BETWEEN SETS

**Technique:**
1. Deep inhale through the nose (near-maximal lung inflation)
2. Second brief inhale through the nose (tops off lungs, reinflates collapsed alveoli)
3. Long, slow exhale through the mouth until lungs are completely empty

**When:** Immediately after completing last rep of every set (treat it as "rep 0" of your rest period)

**Effect:** Fastest known method to shift from sympathetic (alert/exertion) to parasympathetic (calm/recovery). Heart rate drops faster. More complete recovery between sets.

---

## 11. POST-WORKOUT DOWN-REGULATION BREATHING

**Duration:** 3-5 minutes after EVERY workout (set a timer)

**Method (any of these):**
- Repeated physiological sighs (double inhale nose, long exhale mouth) for 3 min
- Slow deliberate breathing with emphasis on exhales (make exhales longer than inhales)
- Any pattern that emphasizes active, extended exhales

**Why:** Shifts nervous system from training mode to recovery mode. You get fitter BETWEEN workouts, not during them. This accelerates the adaptation cascade. Reduces cortisol, adrenaline, inflammatory markers post-workout.

**When to do it:** Sitting in car, in shower, standing after workout. Eyes closed if possible.

---

## 12. GRIP & IRRADIATION PRINCIPLE (Pavel Tsatsouline)

**During sets:** Grip the weight as tightly as possible with BOTH hands (even the stationary hand). Contract your core. Fill body with air. This irradiates force generation through the nervous system and increases strength output.

**Between sets:** Do the opposite. Relax hands completely. Relax core. Use physiological sighs. Open fists. Maximize parasympathetic recovery.

---

## 13. HEAT/COLD CONTRAST PROTOCOL (Tuesday - Recovery Day)

**Structure:**
- 3-5 rounds on a single day, once per week
- Each round: ~20 min sauna/hot bath + ~5 min cold plunge/cold shower
- Total time: 75-125 min

**Temperature guidelines:**
- Heat: Hot enough that you sweat and want to get out, but not dangerous
- Cold: Cold enough that you really want to get out (45-50 deg F / 7-10 deg C for experienced users)
- Build tolerance gradually over time

**Growth hormone effect:** Doing multiple sauna sessions (3-5 rounds) on ONE day per week produces up to 16x increase in growth hormone. This effect is NOT seen when sauna is spread across multiple days.

---

## 14. COLD EXPOSURE TIMING - INTERFERENCE EFFECT

**The rule:** Do NOT do ice baths/cold water immersion immediately after strength, hypertrophy, or endurance workouts. It blocks adaptations.

**Safe timing:**
- Cold plunge on a separate day (e.g., Tuesday recovery day)
- Minimum 4 hours after training, ideally 8+ hours
- Cold showers are NOT the same as cold immersion - no evidence they impair adaptations
- Cold showers after training: appears to be fine

---

## 15. HEAT EXPOSURE TIMING

**If doing heat on a training day:** Can do before training. If doing after, skip cold that session or ensure 4-8 hours gap before cold immersion.

**Best practice:** Dedicate one full day (e.g., Tuesday) to heat/cold contrast, separate from all training.

## 16. SOLEUS PUSH-UPS

**Technique:**
1. Sit with knee bent at ~90 degrees (right angle)
2. Push down on your toes and lift your heel 10-15 degrees
3. Contract calf muscle (soleus)
4. Lower heel back down
5. Repeat continuously

**Duration studied:** Up to 270 min (4.5 hours) throughout the day while seated

**Effort level:** Light - do NOT contract the muscle hard. Gentle, continuous movement.

**Results from University of Houston study (published in iScience):**
- 52% reduction in post-meal blood glucose spikes
- 60% reduction in hyperinsulinemia (excess insulin)
- Improved systemic metabolic regulation
- Benefits lasted 2+ hours after a meal
- Soleus = only 1% of total musculature, yet systemic metabolic effects

**Use case:** While seated at desk, on Zoom calls, on planes, in class. Zero cost, zero equipment.

---

## 17. STATIC STRETCHING

**Hold time:** 30-60 seconds per stretch
**Intensity:** ~60% of maximum stretch (do NOT push to maximum range)
**Technique:** Exhale and relax midsection. Mentally and physically relax into the stretch.
**Frequency:** 2-3x per week (daily is ideal but 3x is sufficient)
**Timing:** Evening preferred (separate from morning training). Can be done at standing desk, watching TV, before bed.
**Target muscles:** Quadriceps, hamstrings, lats, shoulders, back

---

## 18. FASTED VS. FED TRAINING

**No universal rule.** Determine what works for you individually.

**Huberman's approach:**
- Prefers fasted for both cardio and resistance training
- First meal typically ~11:00 AM
- Night before heavy training: eat starch/carbs (rice, pasta) to load muscle glycogen
- After training: protein + starch within ~1 hour
- If hungry in morning: small meal of protein + fat (whey protein, Brazil nuts, small oatmeal) is fine
- Better to train fed than to skip training

**Minimum gap if training fed:** 60-90 minutes after eating (ideally 3-4 hours)

---

## 19. SLEEP DEPRIVATION - TRAIN OR SKIP?

**Poor sleep (3-4 hours):**
- Option A: Skip training. Slide workout to next day.
- Option B: Do 10-30 min (ideally 30-60 min) NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest), then train

**Terrible sleep or high stress:** Skip entirely. Training while truly depleted increases illness risk, which costs multiple training days.

**After any layoff (illness, travel, etc.):** Ramp up over 1-2 weeks. Do NOT jump back to full intensity.

---

## 20. TRAINING WHILE SICK

- **Feeling slightly off / tiny sniffle:** Train at reduced volume. Cut workout 15 min short. Reduce sets to failure from 30% to ~10%. Throttle endurance by 10-20%.
- **Real cold/flu symptoms:** Do NOT train. Wait until fully recovered.
- **Return from illness:** 1-2 weeks of gradual ramp-up to full intensity

## 21. EXERCISE SNACKS

### Cardiovascular Snack
- **100 jumping jacks** (30-90 sec) - full form, hands touch overhead, legs wide
- **20-30 sec stair climbing** as fast as safely possible
- **20-30 sec sprint to your car** (not all-out)
- No warmup needed. Insert randomly throughout the day.
- Frequency: 3-5x per week

### Muscular Endurance Snack
- **Wall sit:** 30-60+ sec (to near-failure, not muscular failure)
- **Plank:** Hold as long as comfortable (can do during phone calls)
- **Max push-ups:** Chest to ground, full extension, piston-like, no pausing at top. Track your number over time.
- Frequency: 3-5x per week

**Purpose:** Maintain or enhance fitness. Especially valuable during busy weeks when regular training is disrupted. Does NOT impede other training.

## 22. THE LINE - WORKOUT FOCUS PROTOCOL

1. Pick a physical location at the threshold of your workout space (gym entrance, trailhead, etc.)
2. Once you cross that line: all business. No texting, no social media scrolling.
3. Pre-select your playlist/podcast/audiobook BEFORE crossing the line.
4. Stay with that selection for the entire workout.
5. When you cross back over the line: workout is over, transition to recovery.

## 23. SUPPLEMENTS FOR FITNESS

### Creatine Monohydrate
- **Form:** Creatine monohydrate (cheapest AND most effective)
- **No loading phase needed.** Just take daily.
- **Timing:** Any time of day (Huberman takes post-workout by habit, but timing does not matter)
- **Dosing by body weight:**
  - 130-180 lbs: **5 grams/day**
  - 185-250 lbs: **10-15 grams/day**
- **Note:** The standard "5g for everyone" advice is not well-calibrated. Larger individuals need more.
- **Delivery:** Mix into whey protein shake or water with lemon juice

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA)
- **Target:** 1-2 grams of EPA per day
- **Source:** Liquid fish oil (most cost-effective) or fish oil capsules
- **Tip:** Lemon-flavored liquid fish oil offsets taste

### Rhodiola Rosea
- **Dose:** 100-200 mg (100 mg likely sufficient)
- **Timing:** 10-20 minutes before high-intensity workouts only (2-3x per week)
- **NOT for:** Easy runs or Zone 2 cardio
- **Effect:** Cortisol modulator. Reduces perceived exertion during training. Reduces post-workout energy crash. Faster subjective recovery.

## 24. PROTEIN REQUIREMENTS

- Eat protein after training (within ~1 hour for resistance training)
- Earlier meals: lean toward protein + fibrous carbs (meat + salad)
- Later meals: lean toward starch + vegetables (pasta, rice) to support sleep
- After high-intensity resistance training: include starchy carbs + some simple sugars to replenish glycogen

---

## 25. DELOAD / EXTENDED REST

- Every 12-16 weeks: take 1 full week off from structured training
- During deload week: hiking, walking, light recreational activity only
- After deload: gradual ramp-up over first week back

---

## 26. NECK TRAINING

- **Frequency:** 2x per week (Wednesday + Saturday)
- **Method:** Wrap a plate in a towel. Place on head. Move neck front-to-back and side-to-side.
- **Do NOT do:** Neck bridges (disc damage risk - sudden, not gradual)
- **Weight:** Light to moderate. Goal is stability, not necessarily hypertrophy.
- **Purpose:** Injury prevention (car accidents, falls), posture (prevents forward chin), reduces shoulder issues and headaches

---

## 27. CAFFEINE & TRAINING

- Standard rule: Delay caffeine 90-120 min after waking (only if afternoon crashes are an issue)
- **Exception:** If training in the morning, take caffeine within 30-60 min of waking - before the workout
- Hydrate + electrolytes + caffeine = pre-training stack
