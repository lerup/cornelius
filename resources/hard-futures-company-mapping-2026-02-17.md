## I. COMPUTE

### 1. Sovereign Processors - Novel Chip Architectures

**RISC-V & Novel Architectures:**

- **Openchip** (Spain) - RISC-V SoC and accelerators for AI/HPC, integrating proprietary high-performance cores
- **Semidynamics** (Spain) - RISC-V multicore environments with tensor units, eliminating need for separate NPUs/GPUs
- **SiPearl** (France) - Europe's first out-of-order RISC-V processor (eProcessor project), Intel 3 node deployment
- **Axelera AI** (Netherlands) - Edge AI accelerators leveraging RISC-V for machine vision
- **BrainChip** (established but active in Europe) - Neuromorphic RISC-V edge AI solutions

**Neuromorphic Computing:**

- **Innatera** (Netherlands) - Spiking Neural Processor T1, RISC-V neuromorphic microcontroller, 500x energy savings vs CPU, <100x latency reduction, NATO DIANA 2025
- **SynSense** (Switzerland) - Neuromorphic vision sensors and processors, low-power edge AI
- **Rain Neuromorphics** (UK) - Photonic neuromorphic processors using light for AI computation

**Validation:** Multiple companies selected for European Processor Initiative and received €270M EU investment in RISC-V architecture

### 2. Cryptographic Hardware - Post-Quantum & Trusted Execution

**Post-Quantum Cryptography:**

- **PQShield** (UK) - Oxford spinout, $37M Series B (2024), hardware/firmware for embedded devices, first approved by US NRC, crypto SDK for mobile/server
- **Crypto Quantique** (UK) - End-to-end IoT security platform, quantum-random number generators, London-based
- **ID Quantique** (Switzerland) - Industry leader since 2001, quantum-safe crypto, random number generators, Geneva University spinout
- **Quantum Blockchains** (Poland) - Quantum-safe cryptography for blockchain, Pulawy-based, founded 2018

**Validation:** PQShield and ID Quantique are European leaders with significant US and EU government contracts

- **Oxford Quantum Circuits** (UK) - Superconducting quantum computers, commercial cloud access

**Neutral Atoms:**

- **PASQAL** (France) - $140M raised (Temasek, Aramco, EIC), 100+ qubit "Orion" deployed at CEA-GENCI and Jülich, founded 2019
- **Planqc** (Germany) - Neutral atom quantum computing, Munich-based

**Trapped Ions:**

- **Alpine Quantum Technologies** (Austria) - University of Innsbruck spinout, trapped ion systems
- **Universal Quantum** (UK) - Trapped ion quantum computing, Brighton-based

**Photonic:**

- **Quandela** (France) - Photonic quantum computers, Paris-Saclay
- **ORCA Computing** (UK) - Photonic quantum computing integrated with telecom infrastructure

**Validation:** IQM and PASQAL are best-funded European quantum companies with active EU supercomputer deployments

- **Isar Aerospace** (Germany) - "Spectrum" rocket, first orbital launch attempt March 2024 (failed on attitude control), largest European NewSpace company

## IV. ENERGY

### 13. Fusion Energy - All Approaches

**Stellarator:**

- **Proxima Fusion** (Germany) - €130M Series A (June 2025), Europe's largest private fusion investment, quasi-isodynamic stellarators, Max Planck Institute spinout, €20M seed (April 2024)
- **Renaissance Fusion** (France) - €32M raised, high-temperature superconductor stellarator design
- **Type One Energy** (US-based but EU collaboration) - "Infinity Two" 350MW plant planned with TVA, stellarator design

**Inertial Confinement:**

- **Marvel Fusion** (Germany) - €385M total funding (€170M private, €215M public), Europe's best-funded fusion company, Series B extension €50M (March 2025), laser-based nano-structured fuel, founded 2019
- **Focused Energy** (Germany, US-based collaboration) - Inertial fusion energy with high-power lasers

**Other Approaches:**

- **Gauss Fusion** (Germany) - Magnetic confinement, gigawatt-class power plant architect
- **Commonwealth Fusion Systems** (US, collaboration with EU) - Tokamak SPARC design

**Validation:** European fusion startups raised record funding in 2025, Marvel Fusion + Proxima exceed €500M combined capital, commercial viability timeline 2030s

## METHODOLOGY NOTES

**Company Selection Criteria:**
1. European HQ (EU27, UK, Switzerland, Norway, Israel with EU operations)
2. Founded after 2015 (exceptions for critical infrastructure: Exail 1960, PAL Robotics 2004)
3. Raised institutional VC or received government program funding (NATO DIANA, EIC, SPRIND, ESA BIC)
4. Active development as of February 2026

**Data Limitations:**
- Stealth-mode companies (especially defense) not captured unless publicly announced
- Some NATO DIANA and EIC winners not yet disclosed company names
- Revenue/employee data often unavailable for private companies
- Israeli companies included if clear European operational presence (ICEYE, Exodigo)

**Research Coverage:**
- 24 searches across capability areas
- 10+ searches across VC portfolios and government programs
- 150+ companies identified and validated
