The origins of venture

1957 — Arthur Rock backed eight scientists who walked out of Shockley Semiconductor. No business plan. No pitch deck. $1.38M from Fairchild Camera. That bet created Intel, AMD, and the entire semiconductor industry.
1976 — Mike Markkula drove to a garage and wrote a $250,000 check for a third of Apple. Two kids and a circuit board. No IC committee. No consensus.
Every legendary venture return came from the same place: one person's conviction about something most people could not yet evaluate.
Image: Fairchild Semiconductor founding team, Mountain View, 1957
