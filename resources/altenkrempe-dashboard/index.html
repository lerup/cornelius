Reetdachhaus Altenkrempe - Investment Model

How the Model Works

Step 1: Total Acquisition Cost

Buying a property in Germany costs more than the purchase price. You pay transfer tax (Grunderwerbsteuer, 6.5% in Schleswig-Holstein) and notary + land registry fees (~1.8%). These are sunk costs - they don't increase the property's resale value.

Total Acquisition = Purchase Price + Transfer Tax + Notary Fees
Example: 800,000 + 52,000 + 14,400 = 866,400

Step 2: Equity & Loan

You split the total acquisition cost between your own money (equity) and a bank loan. The more you borrow, the less equity you need - but you pay interest. The freed-up cash goes into a separate investment portfolio.

Equity = Total Acquisition - Loan
Starting Portfolio = Loan Amount (the cash you freed up)
866,400 - 500,000 = 366,400 equity, 500,000 in portfolio

LTV (Loan-to-Value) = Loan / Purchase Price. It measures leverage. Banks typically cap at 80%.

500,000 / 800,000 = 62.5% LTV

Step 3: Loan Payments (Annuitat)

A German mortgage (Annuitatendarlehen) has a fixed annual payment. Each payment is split between interest (what the bank charges) and principal repayment (Tilgung, which reduces your debt). Early on, most goes to interest. Over time, more goes to principal.

Set by Tilgung: You choose the initial repayment rate. The loan term is computed from this. At 2% Tilgung + 3.5% interest, you pay 5.5% of the loan annually.

Annuity = Loan × (Interest Rate + Tilgung Rate)
Term = ln((r+t)/t) / ln(1+r)
500,000 × 5.5% = 27,500/year. Term: ~29 years

Set by Term: You choose how many years to repay. The annuity is computed.

Annuity = Loan × r / (1 - (1+r)-n)
20-year term at 3.5%: Annuity = ~35,182/year. Tilgung: ~3.5%

Zinsbindung is the fixed-rate period (typically 10 years). After that, you refinance at whatever rates are available. The model shows your remaining balance at that point.

Step 4: Tax Allocation (Deductible Share)

When you use a property for both rental and personal use, the tax office only lets you deduct costs proportional to rental use. Vacant days are split between rental and personal use based on their ratio.

Rental Days = Rental Weeks × 7
Vacancy to Rental = Vacant Days × Rental Days / (Rental + Personal Days)
Deductible Share = (Rental Days + Vacancy to Rental) / 365
25 rental weeks, 5 personal: Deductible Share = 83.3% (rounded to 80% conservatively)

Step 5: Operating Costs

Total Cash Costs = everything you actually pay out per year: management fee, maintenance, insurance, energy, taxes, reserves. This is cash out of your pocket.

Tax-Deductible Costs = the portion the tax office accepts as an expense. Different rules apply:

Management Fee: 100% deductible (direct rental cost)
Regular costs (maintenance, insurance, etc.): deductible at Deductible Share %
Zweitwohnungsteuer: 0% (personal tax, not deductible)
Capex Reserve: 0% (only deductible when actually spent)
Total cash costs: 27,650. Tax-deductible: 22,520

Step 6: Depreciation (AfA)

The building (not the land) loses value on paper. You can deduct this "loss" (AfA = Absetzung fur Abnutzung) from your rental income even though no cash leaves your account. For pre-1925 buildings: 2.5% per year of the building's acquisition value.

Building Value = Total Acquisition × Building Share %
Annual AfA = Building Value × 2.5%
Deductible AfA = Annual AfA × Deductible Share
866,400 × 65% = 563,160. AfA = 14,079. Deductible: 11,263

Step 7: Taxable Income & Tax Effect

Your rental taxable income = Revenue minus all deductible costs, AfA, and interest. If this is negative (a "tax loss"), it reduces your other income (salary) and saves you taxes at your marginal rate. If positive, you owe additional tax.

Taxable Income = Revenue - Deductible Costs - Deductible AfA - Deductible Interest
Tax Effect = -Taxable Income × Tax Rate
(positive = saving, negative = tax due)
40,000 - 22,520 - 11,263 - 14,000 = -7,783 (loss)
Tax saving: 7,783 × 42% = +3,269

Step 8: Annual Net Cash Flow

What actually flows through your bank account each year.

Net Cash = Revenue - Total Cash Costs - Loan Payment + Tax Effect
40,000 - 27,650 - 27,500 + 3,269 = -11,881

Negative cash flow means the property costs you money each year. This shortfall is funded from the portfolio.

Step 9: Portfolio Growth

The EUR 500K you didn't put into the property is invested (e.g., in a diversified ETF portfolio). Each year it grows at the portfolio return rate, then the property's cash shortfall is withdrawn.

Portfolio = Previous Portfolio × (1 + Return) + Net Cash Flow
Year 1: 500,000 × 1.05 - 11,881 = 513,119

Step 10: Total Wealth

After N years, your total wealth = property value minus remaining loan plus portfolio.

Property Value = Purchase Price × (1 + Appreciation)years
Property Equity = Property Value - Loan Balance
Total Wealth = Property Equity + Portfolio

Return Composition

The total gain comes from four sources:

Appreciation = Property value at end minus purchase price. This is your capital gain on the real estate.

Portfolio Growth = Portfolio at end minus starting portfolio. What your freed-up capital earned in the market.

Equity Buildup = Loan amount minus remaining loan balance. The portion of your loan payments that went to principal (forced savings).

Tax Savings = Sum of all annual tax effects. The money saved by offsetting rental losses against salary income.

Operating Deficit = Sum of (Revenue - Total Costs - Annuity) before tax. The pre-tax shortfall. This is the price you pay for the property's operating deficit. It includes the sunk Nebenkosten.

Total Gain = Appreciation + Portfolio Growth + Equity Buildup + Tax Savings + Operating Deficit
