# Meituan (3690.HK) - Investment Research Report

**Date**: 2026-02-21
**Purpose**: Demand pipeline and revenue visibility analysis for value investing memo
**Status**: WARNING - Active investment thesis faces a structural shock from a multi-party subsidy war

---

## Executive Summary

Meituan entered 2025 as one of China's most profitable internet platforms - RMB 35.8 billion net profit in 2024, dominant market position, narrowing new-initiative losses, and aggressive buybacks. By Q3 2025, it had swung to an RMB 18.6 billion quarterly loss, its stock had fallen over 30%, and management issued a full-year 2025 loss guidance of RMB 23.3-24.3 billion [1][2].

The proximate cause is a three-way subsidy war ignited by JD.com's February 2025 entry into food delivery, amplified by Alibaba's 50-billion-yuan subsidy program via Taobao Instant Commerce. Combined industry losses across the three platforms exceeded RMB 100 billion in 2025 [3]. The government intervened in July-August 2025, and all parties pledged to curb irrational competition, but Meituan warned losses will persist into Q1 2026.

**The core question for a value memo**: Is this a cyclical earnings trough caused by a temporary price war - or the opening phase of permanent market share erosion? The evidence points toward the former, but with meaningful uncertainty. Meituan's order volume hit all-time records (150M daily orders in July 2025) even while profits collapsed, suggesting the underlying demand machine is intact. What is being destroyed is unit economics, not user engagement [4].

### Segment Acceleration vs. Deceleration

**Decelerating (2025):**
- Core local commerce revenue: slowed from +20% (2024) to +7.7% (Q2 2025) as subsidies destroyed commission economics
- Food delivery commission revenue: declining as AOV falls under subsidy pressure

**Accelerating:**
- New Initiatives revenue: +22.8% in Q2 2025, +15.9% in Q3 2025 - driven by Meituan Instashopping and grocery retail
- International (Keeta): not yet material to group revenue but growing rapidly

**The deceleration is severe but mechanically explainable**: selling and marketing expenses as a percentage of revenue jumped from 19.2% to 35.9% in Q3 2025. Revenue grew 2%, but S&M spend nearly doubled (+90.9%) to RMB 34.3 billion [10]. This is a cost structure shock, not a demand structure shock.

## 3. Competitive Dynamics

### Market Share (Food Delivery, as of mid-2025)

| Platform | Estimated Share | Orders Source | Key Move |
|----------|----------------|---------------|----------|
| Meituan | 55-60% (down from 65-70%) | 70M-90M normal daily; 150M peak | Defending via subsidies + rider pension program |
| Alibaba (Ele.me + Taobao Flash) | 30-35% (Taobao overtook Meituan on daily orders briefly in August) | Surged above Meituan for first time Aug 8 [3] | RMB 50B subsidy program; rebranded as Taobao Instant Commerce |
| JD.com | 5-8% | Entered Feb 2025 | Triggered the war; ~30% subsidy on orders |

Sources: [3][11][12]

### Douyin's Retreat from Food Delivery

This is a key nuance: Douyin did NOT win the food delivery war. After entering with 15% estimated share in Q1 2024, Douyin pivoted away from food delivery toward group-purchase-to-dine-in (in-store visits without delivery). By 2025, Douyin Life Services repositioned around "buy online, use in-store" rather than competing in last-mile delivery logistics [13]. This is a meaningful positive for Meituan - the competitor with the most distribution reach (Douyin's 600M+ DAU) chose not to fight on logistics economics.

### Key Competitive Insight

Meituan's structural moat is its rider network - 9M+ active delivery riders, proprietary routing AI, and density-based economics. JD.com and Alibaba are spending heavily to match this but face: (1) fixed infrastructure costs, (2) lower density outside tier-1 cities, and (3) merchant relationships Meituan built over a decade. JD's food delivery push appears motivated more by strategic aggression than genuine profitability conviction.

Goldman Sachs noted Meituan maintains superior unit economics leadership versus competitors even during the subsidy war [14].

## 4. AI Investment and Strategy

### Scale

- Wang Xing stated the company "invested billions in GPU resources" in 2024-2025, and characterized GPU acquisition as the top priority over the past year [15]
- R&D spending reached RMB 6.9 billion in Q3 2025 alone, +31% YoY [10]
- LongCat (internal LLM) API usage rose from 10% to 68% of internal calls in one year [16]

### Three-Pillar AI Strategy (Wang Xing's framework)

1. **AI at Work**: Operational efficiency - intelligent customer service (+20% efficiency), AI sales assistant (-44% workload), AI-generated code now at 27% of new code
2. **AI in Products**: Consumer-facing features embedded in delivery, search, recommendations
3. **Building LLMs**: LongCat model development to compete with ByteDance Doubao and Alibaba Qwen

### Autonomous Delivery

- Drone delivery: 450,000 commercial tasks completed by end 2024; 54th commercial route launched in Hong Kong March 2025
- Autonomous vehicles: 4.9 million commercial orders fulfilled by end-2024
- Drone team reports directly to Wang Xing since recent reorganization [17]
- Embodied AI: 30+ investments in robotics/AI startups; recent focused investments in Independent Variable Robotics, TARS, Xinghaitu [18]

### Margin Impact

AI investment is pressuring near-term margins but management argues it is the key to long-term delivery cost reduction. Autonomous delivery economics, if achieved at scale, would radically reduce the rider cost structure which currently dominates delivery economics.

## 6. Regulatory Environment

### 2025 Key Regulatory Developments

**July 2025**: State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR) summoned Meituan, Ele.me, and JD.com for talks on fair competition and food safety compliance [20]

**August 2025**: All three platforms jointly pledged to:
- Eliminate improper promotional tactics
- Ensure fair merchant access to promotions
- Improve rider welfare and protections
- Promote healthier balance between delivery and dine-in [21]

**Ongoing**: New Anti-Unfair Competition Law revisions under review; would cap fees charged to restaurants and mandate greater transparency around subsidy campaigns

**September 2025**: Meituan and JD shares rose after China announced moves to defuse the food delivery war - signals government intent to force truce [22]

### Assessment

The regulatory environment in 2025 is actually more favorable than 2021-2022, when Meituan faced an RMB 3.4 billion antitrust fine and mandatory business practice restructuring. The current regulatory posture is focused on cooling irrational competition rather than punishing the dominant platform. This is a net positive for Meituan's long-term stability.

## 7. Chinese Consumer Environment

### Macro Context

Chinese consumer confidence remains structurally weak [23]. Key headwinds:
- Real estate sector down 50%+ from peak in sales and investment since 2021
- Local government fiscal deficits constraining stimulus effectiveness
- Household savings rate elevated as precautionary buffer

### Meituan-Specific Dynamics

Despite macro weakness, food delivery shows resilience as:
1. Value-for-money positioning benefits from subsidy programs drawing in new cohort of users
2. Service sector retail growing faster than goods retail (5.1% service growth Jan-Aug 2025)
3. Meituan serves 770M users with 14.5M active merchants - near-universal urban China penetration

**The paradox**: Volume records are being set in an environment of consumer weakness - because subsidies are pulling forward demand and training new user habits. When subsidies normalize, the structural question is how much of this demand sticks vs. evaporates.

## Key Risks

**Bull Case Risks (things that would validate the thesis faster)**:
- Government forces a credible truce in H1 2026, subsidies normalize by mid-year
- Meituan's 100B investment program attracts enough merchants to maintain structural advantages
- Keeta scales rapidly in Middle East, becomes a visible new earnings engine by 2027-2028

**Bear Case Risks (thesis-breaking)**:
1. **Permanent market share loss**: If Taobao Instant Commerce locks in 30-35% share at normalized subsidy levels, Meituan's take-rate and commission income may never fully recover
2. **Duration risk**: If the subsidy war extends through 2026, the cash burn and balance sheet deterioration become more severe; RMB 168B cash buffer sounds large but is being consumed at ~RMB 24B/year net loss pace
3. **AI disruption to local services**: A future where AI agents book restaurants and services without platform intermediation would reduce Meituan's traffic value
4. **Wang Xing key-man risk**: His direct involvement in drone/AI strategy and overall vision makes executive continuity material

**Non-consensus concern**: Meituan's own SVP said the order surge is "mostly a bubble." If subsidized demand leaves when subsidies normalize, the company will have spent RMB 100B+ defending volume that doesn't stick, and will need to rebuild unit economics from a worse market share position.

## Investment Relevance Summary

**The memo should address one central question**: What does Meituan look like in 2027 when the dust settles?

If Meituan exits the subsidy war with 60%+ food delivery share, profitability restored in new initiatives, Keeta contributing $1B+ in GMV, and AI reducing per-delivery costs by 15-20% - this is a business worth considerably more than current prices imply.

The 2024 baseline - RMB 337.6B revenue, RMB 35.8B net profit, growing 22% - proves the unit economics exist. The question is whether 2024 was the peak or the baseline.

Cash position (RMB 168B at end-2024) provides 7+ years of 2025-level losses before existential balance sheet pressure. The company is not in crisis. It is in a war, with a strong balance sheet, a structurally superior delivery network, and a government-forced truce already signaled.
